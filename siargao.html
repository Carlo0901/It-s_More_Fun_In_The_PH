Siargao

The Surfing Capital of the Philippines

Siargao

is a stunning tear-shaped island located in the province of Surigao del Norte, in the northeastern part of Mindanao, Philippines. Known as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," it has gained worldwide popularity among surfers due to its world-class waves and laid-back island vibe. The island boasts a diverse range of activities and attractions for tourists, including surfing, island hopping, rock pools, lagoons, caves, and abundant marine life. Cloud 9, a world-renowned surf spot, is a reef break that offers powerful and hollow waves, attracting surfers from around the world. Siargao is surrounded by numerous small islands, each offering unique attractions such as hidden lagoons, white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Other popular destinations on the island include the Magpupungko Rock Pools, a natural wonder that reveals crystal-clear waters during low tide, and the Sugba Lagoon, a tranquil body of water surrounded by lush vegetation and towering limestone cliffs. The island's relaxed and laid-back atmosphere is reflected in its beachfront accommodations, restaurants, and bars. Siargao's natural beauty, friendly locals, and world-class surf breaks make it a must-visit destination in the Philippines for surfers, nature lovers, and adventure seekers.

Wander to Siargao through Air, Sea and Land

By Plane:

The fastest and most convenient way to get to Siargao is by plane. You can book a flight from Manila, Cebu, or Davao to Siargao Island Airport. Major airlines such as Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and Skyjet offer daily flights to Siargao.

By Ferry:

If you're traveling from Surigao City, you can take a ferry to Siargao Island. The ferry ride takes around 3 to 4 hours, depending on the weather conditions. You can take a ferry from the Port of Surigao to the Port of Dapa or the Port of Del Carmen.

By Sea and Land:

If you're coming from other parts of Mindanao, you can take a bus or van to Butuan City or Surigao City, and then take a ferry to Siargao Island.

Once you arrive in Siargao, you can take a tricycle, motorcycle, or van to your accommodation or your desired destination on the island. Siargao is a small island, and most places are easily accessible by motorbike or tricycle.
