Cebu

Queen City of the South

Cebu

is a province located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. It is the oldest city in the country and is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, stunning beaches, and breathtaking natural wonders. One of the top tourist destinations in Cebu is the city of Cebu, which is also known as the "Queen City of the South." The city is home to many historical landmarks, including the Basilica del Santo Niño, a 16th-century church that houses the oldest religious relic in the country, and the Magellan's Cross, which was planted by Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan in 1521. Cebu is also known for its beaches, with many popular destinations such as Mactan Island, Malapascua Island, and Bantayan Island. These beaches offer crystal-clear waters, pristine white sand, and various water activities such as diving, snorkeling, and island hopping. Apart from its beaches, Cebu has many natural wonders, including the Osmeña Peak, which is the highest peak in the province and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, and the Kawasan Falls, a three-tiered waterfall located in the town of Badian. Cebu is also famous for its food, with many popular dishes such as lechon (roasted pig), dried mangoes, and Cebu-style chorizo. The province is also home to many festivals, including the Sinulog Festival, which is a grand celebration in honor of the Santo Niño held every January. In summary, Cebu is a province that offers a mix of history, culture, stunning beaches, breathtaking natural wonders, and delicious cuisine. It is a must-visit destination for those looking for a diverse and exciting travel experience in the Philippines.

Wander to Cebu through Air, Sea and Land

By Plane:

Cebu has an international airport, Mactan-Cebu International Airport (CEB), which is located on Mactan Island, just a short distance from Cebu City. You can take a direct flight to Cebu from many cities in the Philippines and around the world.

By Ferry:

Cebu has several ports that offer ferry services to nearby islands and cities, such as Bohol, Dumaguete, and Manila. The most popular ports in Cebu are Cebu City Pier 1 and Cebu City Pier 3.

By Sea and Land:

If you're coming from other parts of the Philippines, you can take a bus or a van to reach Cebu. The journey will involve a combination of land and sea travel, as you'll need to take a ferry from nearby islands to reach Cebu. Some popular routes include Cebu to Bohol, Cebu to Dumaguete, and Cebu to Leyte.

By Private transfer:

You can also arrange for a private transfer, such as a car or a van, to take you from your starting point to Cebu. This can be a convenient and comfortable option if you're traveling with a group or with lots of luggage.

Once you arrive in Cebu, there are many transportation options available, including taxis, jeepneys, and buses, to help you explore the city and its surrounding areas.
