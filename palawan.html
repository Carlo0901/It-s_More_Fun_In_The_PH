Palawan

Last Ecological Frontier

Palawan

is a province located in the western part of the Philippines, known for its stunning natural beauty, pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life. It is the largest province in the country in terms of land area and is often referred to as the "last ecological frontier" due to its rich biodiversity and numerous conservation efforts. The main tourist attractions in Palawan are El Nido, Coron, and Puerto Princesa. El Nido is a municipality located in the northern part of Palawan, famous for its beautiful lagoons, limestone cliffs, and pristine beaches. It is a popular destination for island hopping, snorkeling, and diving. Some of the notable attractions in El Nido include the Big Lagoon, Small Lagoon, Secret Lagoon, and Nacpan Beach. Coron, on the other hand, is a municipality located in the northern part of Palawan, known for its stunning lagoons, shipwrecks, and coral gardens. It is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling, with numerous dive sites that offer a glimpse into the rich marine biodiversity of the area. Some of the popular attractions in Coron include the Kayangan Lake, Twin Lagoon, and WWII shipwrecks. Puerto Princesa, the capital city of Palawan, is known for its underground river, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature. The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a navigable river that flows through a cave system, showcasing spectacular rock formations and unique flora and fauna. Apart from these main attractions, Palawan also offers numerous other natural wonders such as the Honda Bay, Port Barton, and Tubbataha Reef, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular diving spot. Palawan is truly a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those who want to experience the beauty of the Philippines.

Wander to Palawan through Air, Sea and Land

By Air:

The easiest and most convenient way to get to Palawan is by air. Several airlines operate flights to Palawan's main airport, which is located in Puerto Princesa City. You can book a flight from major cities in the Philippines like Manila, Cebu, and Davao, or from international destinations such as Hong Kong, Taipei, and Seoul. From the airport, you can take a taxi or van to your hotel or destination.

By Sea:

Palawan is also accessible by sea. There are several ferry services that operate from Manila and other ports in the Philippines to different parts of Palawan, such as Coron and El Nido. You can also take a cruise that includes Palawan as part of its itinerary.

By Sea and Land:

If you are traveling from other parts of the Philippines, you can take a bus or drive to ports in Mindoro or Batangas, and then take a ferry or fastcraft to Palawan.

Once you arrive in Palawan, you can hire a van or take public transportation to explore the different attractions on the island. It's recommended to book your travel arrangements in advance, especially during peak season, to ensure availability and avoid any inconvenience.
