Vigan

Heritage City of the Philippines

Vigan

is a city located in the northern part of the Philippines, specifically in the province of Ilocos Sur. It is known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, which has earned it a spot on the UNESCO World Heritage List. One of the top tourist destinations in Vigan is Calle Crisologo, a cobblestone street lined with centuries-old buildings that have been well-preserved and restored. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the street, admire the beautiful colonial architecture, and shop for local souvenirs and handicrafts. Another popular attraction in Vigan is the St. Paul's Metropolitan Cathedral, a beautiful church with a Baroque-style facade and a soaring bell tower. The church dates back to the 18th century and is one of the oldest in the country. Visitors can also explore the numerous museums in Vigan, including the Crisologo Museum, which is dedicated to the life and legacy of the prominent Crisologo family, and the Syquia Mansion, which was once the home of the former Philippine President Elpidio Quirino. Aside from the historical and cultural attractions, Vigan is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes popular dishes such as empanada (a fried pastry filled with meat, egg, and vegetables), bagnet (deep-fried crispy pork belly), and Vigan longganisa (a type of sausage made from ground pork). In summary, Vigan is a city that offers a unique and charming glimpse into the Spanish colonial past of the Philippines. It is a must-visit destination for those interested in history, culture, and architecture, as well as those looking to sample some of the country's most delicious cuisine.

Wander to Bohol through Air, Sea and Land

By Plane:

The nearest airport to Vigan is the Laoag International Airport, which is about 80 kilometers away. Several airlines offer daily flights to Laoag from Manila. From Laoag, you can take a bus or hire a van to Vigan.

By Bus:

There are several bus companies that offer direct trips to Vigan from Manila, such as Partas, Dominion Bus Lines, and Viron Transit. The trip takes about 8-9 hours and passes through the scenic countryside of northern Luzon.

By Car:

If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car in Manila and take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) to the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX), then take the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX) all the way to Vigan. The trip takes around 8-10 hours, depending on traffic.

By Tour:

If you prefer a hassle-free trip, you can also book a tour package that includes transportation, accommodations, and guided tours of Vigan's top attractions.
